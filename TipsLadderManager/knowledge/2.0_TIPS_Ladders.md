## TIPS Ladder Terminology

**[DARA (Desired Annual Real Amount)](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#dara):** Target total cash flow for a funded year in real (inflation-adjusted) terms
- Replaces DAA from 2.0 Bond Ladders
- "Real" means in today's dollars

**[ARA (Annual Real Amount)](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#ara):** Actual cash flow produced by the ladder for a funded year in real terms
- Replaces AA from 2.0 Bond Ladders
- May differ from DARA due to rounding

**Note on "Real":** All principal and interest values are inflation-adjusted, so "real" is implied throughout

## Gap Years

**[Gap Years](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#gap-years):** Years within the ladder period where no TIPS exist (currently 2037, 2038, 2039).

**Synthetic TIPS for Gap Years:**
Created to maintain ladder continuity and facilitate duration matching.
- **Maturity**: January 15 of the gap year (10-year TIPS are issued in January and July).
- **Index ratio**: 1.0 (assumes refCPI = dated refCPI).
- **Price**: 100 (par).

**Gap Parameter Calculation:**
Synthetic TIPS are processed **longest to shortest** to maintain a running LMI pool from already-sized gap rungs:
1. **Anchors**: `anchorBefore` (latest Jan TIPS before gap) and `anchorAfter` (earliest Feb TIPS after gap).
2. **Interpolate Yield**: Linear interpolation between anchors using maturity dates.
3. **Synthetic Coupon**: `MAX(0.00125, FLOOR(yield * 100 / 0.125) * 0.00125)`.
4. **Duration**: Calculate Modified Duration for each synthetic bond.
5. **PI per Synthetic**: `1,000 * (1 + coupon / 2)` (nPeriods = 1, Jan maturity).
6. **Required Quantity**: `qty_y = ROUND((DARA - runningLMI) / (PI_y / 1,000))`.
7. **LMI Update**: `runningLMI += qty_y * 1,000 * coupon` (for the next shorter rung).

**Gap Total Cost**: The aggregate theoretical cost of the block: `Σ [ qty_y * 1,000 ]`.
**Gap Avg Duration**: The mean of all synthetic durations in the block.

## Future Years

**Future Years:** Years beyond the last-issued TIPS maturity year (currently 2056). Future years arise when `lastYear > 2056` (max `lastYear` = 2066).

**Hypothetical TIPS for Future Years:**
Processed **longest to shortest** using the longest-dated real TIPS (currently 2056) as a flat-curve anchor:
- **Anchor**: Coupon and yield of the 2056 TIPS.
- **Maturity**: February 15 of the future year (30-year TIPS are issued in February).
- **Index ratio / Price**: 1.0 / 100 (par assumptions).
- **Duration**: Calculate Modified Duration for each hypothetical bond.
- **Quantity and Cost**: Calculated identically to Gap Years (see above) using the flat-curve anchor.

**Future Total Cost**: The aggregate theoretical cost of the future block.
**Future Avg Duration**: The mean of all hypothetical durations in the block.

## Duration Matching (2-Bracket)

Missing blocks (Gap or Future) are covered by additional holdings in a **bracket pair** (or **cover pair**). The goal is for the excess holdings to change in value identically to the missing block if rates move.

**Weight Formula:**
```
lowerWeight * d_lower + upperWeight * d_upper = averageBlockDuration
upperWeight = (averageBlockDuration - d_lower) / (d_upper - d_lower)
lowerWeight = 1 - upperWeight
```

**Fallback (Duration Gap)**: If the block duration exceeds the longest available duration (`averageBlockDuration > d_upper`), all excess is allocated to the upper bracket (`upperWeight = 1.0`).

**Excess Quantities**:
```
upperExQty = ROUND(totalBlockCost * upperWeight / costPerUpperTips)
lowerExQty = ROUND(totalBlockCost * lowerWeight / costPerLowerTips)
```

Duration matching must be educational and traceable. The UI must provide:
1. **Individual Disclosure**: Every synthetic/hypothetical bond used to calculate the average must be listed with its own Modified Duration.
2. **Derivation**: The "Average Duration" must be explicitly shown as the mean of the listed bonds.
3. **Fulcrum Visualization**: A visual representation showing the bracket durations flanking the average duration.
4. **Cost-to-Quantity Chain**: A breakdown showing how the block's total cost is weighted and then converted to discrete bond units.

## Algorithm

Use 2.0 Bond Ladders algorithm with these modifications:

1. **Add:** Identify gap/future years and calculate block parameters (Avg Duration, Total Cost).
2. **Solve**: Determine bracket weights and excess quantities.
3. **Substitute:** Use all inflation-adjusted formulas from 2.1 TIPS Basics.
4. **Sweep**: Process longest-to-shortest, adding bracket excess to the appropriate rungs.
